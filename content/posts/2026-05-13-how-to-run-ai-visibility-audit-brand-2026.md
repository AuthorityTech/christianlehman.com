An AI visibility audit measures whether your brand appears — and how it appears — when buyers ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, or Google AI Mode questions in your category. [McKinsey's October 2025 research](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/growth-marketing-and-sales/our-insights/ai-powered-marketing-and-sales-reach-new-heights-with-agentic-ai) confirms half of consumers now use AI-powered search, and this behavioral shift will influence $750 billion in revenue by 2028. If you have never audited your AI visibility, you are operating blind in the fastest-growing discovery channel in B2B.

[Sona's visibility data](https://www.sona.com/blog/ai-visibility) shows three in four websites are partially or fully invisible to AI engines — even sites with strong organic rankings. This framework covers the seven areas where brands fail in AI discovery and where the fix is usually operational, not creative.

## Why Your SEO Dashboard Is Not Enough

Your Google Search Console data shows rankings. Your analytics show clicks. Neither tells you whether AI engines are citing, recommending, or ignoring your brand when a buyer asks "best [your category] tools" or "how to solve [your problem]."

About [68% of Google searches ended without a click in early 2026](https://sparktoro.com/blog/in-2024-we-saw-the-largest-ever-decline-in-google-search-traffic-to-the-web/), and that number keeps growing as AI-generated answers satisfy the query before anyone reaches your site. [Forrester's 2026 B2B Summit](https://forrester.com/blogs/is-ai-visibility-your-2026-imperative-learn-how-to-achieve-it-at-b2b-summit) identified AI visibility as a strategic imperative because AI answer engines are transforming how B2B buyers research, compare, and evaluate vendors.

Here is the stat that should change how you allocate resources: your own website accounts for [only 5 to 10 percent of what AI search draws from](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/growth-marketing-and-sales/our-insights/ai-powered-marketing-and-sales-reach-new-heights-with-agentic-ai). The remaining 90 to 95 percent comes from third-party sources — publishers, affiliates, reviews, and social platforms. Your SEO dashboard was built for a world where your site was the destination. In AI search, your site is one input among hundreds.

## The 7-Step AI Visibility Audit

### Step 1: Map Your Query Universe Across AI Engines

Start with the 20 to 30 queries your buyers actually ask before purchasing. Not your keyword list — your buyer questions. Then run each one through ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Mode.

[Presenc AI's audit framework](https://presenc.ai/guides/how-to-audit-ai-visibility) recommends beginning with three inputs: your target keywords, your competitor set, and your platform list. I would add a fourth: your buyer's actual language, which often diverges from the terms your marketing team optimizes for.

Record for each query:
- Does your brand appear in the answer?
- Are you cited with a source link?
- Are you recommended, or merely mentioned?
- Which competitor appears instead?

The scale of what you are auditing against matters: ChatGPT now has [700 million weekly active users](https://openai.com/index/), growing four times year over year. Google AI Overviews reach [two billion monthly users across more than 200 countries](https://blog.google/products/search/generative-ai-google-search-may-2025/). And [31 percent of the US population](https://www.emarketer.com/insights/guide-ai/) will use generative AI search in 2026 according to eMarketer. This is not a niche channel.

Run the audit monthly at minimum. AI answers shift as engines recrawl and reindex sources.

### Step 2: Check Crawler Access

This is the most overlooked step and the highest-leverage fix. [Surferstack's 2026 audit checklist](https://surferstack.com/guides/the-ai-search-visibility-audit-10-point-checklist-for-brands-in-2026) puts it plainly: if AI crawlers are blocked, rate-limited, or encountering errors on your site, you are invisible by default.

Check your `robots.txt` for blocks on GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, and GoogleOther. Check your server logs for crawler activity. Check your CDN/WAF rules for rate limits that affect bot traffic. Many enterprise sites block these crawlers without knowing it because security teams treat all bots as threats.

### Step 3: Measure Share of Citation

[Share of citation](https://machinerelations.ai/glossary/share-of-citation) is the percentage of AI-generated answers in your category where your brand is cited as a source. It is the AI-era equivalent of share of voice, and it is the single most diagnostic metric in this audit.

To measure it: take your 20 to 30 buyer queries, run them across engines, count how many times your brand is cited versus how many total citation slots exist. If you appear in 3 out of 30 queries across 4 engines, your share of citation is 3 out of 120 slots — 2.5 percent.

An [IQRush field audit](https://iqrush.com/ai-visibility-audit) found that fewer than one in ten AI-generated answers include the brand, even for companies with strong organic search rankings. Your Google position does not guarantee your AI position.

Here are the benchmarks I use for B2B brands:

| Metric | Weak | Average | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Brand Mention Rate** | Below 30% | 30 to 60% | Above 60% |
| **Owned Domain Citations** | Below 5% | 5 to 15% | Above 15% |
| **Sentiment Accuracy** | Below 70% | 70 to 85% | Above 85% |

Most brands I work with start below 5 percent owned domain citations. The ones winning in AI discovery are above 15 percent. The gap between those numbers is where the audit earns its keep.

### Step 4: Test Entity Resolution

Ask each AI engine: "What is [your company name]?" and "Who founded [your company name]?" The answers reveal whether the engine has resolved your brand as a distinct entity or is confusing you with competitors, products, or unrelated businesses.

Entity clarity depends on consistent naming, structured data, and corroboration across independent sources. If ChatGPT thinks your company does something different from what Perplexity says, your entity signal is fragmented. The fix is usually earned media in authoritative publications that describe your company consistently — which is the core mechanism behind [Machine Relations](https://machinerelations.ai/glossary/machine-relations).

### Step 5: Audit Content Extractability

AI engines do not read your pages the way humans do. They extract structured claims, definitions, comparisons, and data points. [Research from the GEO field](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.08919) confirms this is an evidence-selection problem: engines choose sources that provide clear, attributable, machine-parseable answers.

Run this check on your top 10 pages:
- Does the page answer a specific question in the first 60 words?
- Are key claims stated in standalone, declarative sentences?
- Is structured data present — comparison tables, definition lists, numbered frameworks?
- Are statistics cited with named sources and dates?

Pages that bury the answer below three paragraphs of context-setting prose get skipped by AI engines. Answer-first structure is not a style preference. It is a retrieval requirement.

### Step 6: Assess Earned Media Authority

AI engines weight third-party sources more heavily than brand-owned content. Your own website accounts for only 5 to 10 percent of what AI draws from. The remaining 90 to 95 percent is third-party — and specific platforms dominate.

The [Tinuiti AI Citations Trends Report](https://tinuiti.com/research-insights/ai-citations-trends-report/) found Reddit citations reached 9 percent of all AI citations by January 2026, making Reddit the number-one cited source on Perplexity and top three on SearchGPT and Google AI Mode. Social media citations grew steadily from October 2025 through January 2026, with Reddit as the dominant share.

Audit your earned media footprint:
- How many third-party publications mention your brand in the context of your category?
- Are those publications ones that AI engines actually crawl and cite?
- Is your brand attributed correctly in those placements?
- Do you have earned mentions on Reddit, industry forums, and review platforms?

This is where [citation architecture](https://machinerelations.ai/glossary/citation-architecture) matters: the strategic pattern of earned placements, owned content, and entity signals that compound into AI engine confidence. [Forbes recently called AI visibility auditing essential](https://www.forbes.com/councils/forbesbusinesscouncil/2026/04/24/if-ai-cant-find-you-neither-can-your-customers-how-to-audit-your-brands-ai-visibility/) for any brand that depends on digital discovery.

### Step 7: Benchmark Against Competitors

For each buyer query, note which brands appear. Build a competitive citation map. [Semrush's AI Visibility Index](https://ai-visibility-index.semrush.com/) now tracks AI citation performance across major engines, making competitive benchmarking measurable instead of anecdotal.

Common reasons a competitor outranks you in AI answers:
- They have more earned media citations in publications the engine trusts
- Their content answers the query directly in extractable format
- Their entity signal is clearer and more consistent across sources
- They have structured comparison data that engines prefer to cite

The AI visibility audit tool market has matured rapidly. Platforms like [Semrush](https://ai-visibility-index.semrush.com/), [SE Ranking](https://visible.seranking.com/), and dedicated tools like [Profound](https://www.profound.com/) now track brand mention rate, citation rate, share of voice, and sentiment across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, and Google AI Mode. Pricing ranges from free tiers to $700 per month for monitoring platforms.

## What Each AI Engine Prioritizes

| Engine | Primary signal | Citation behavior | Audit focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ChatGPT** | Training data plus web browsing results | Cites sources when browsing; training data has no attribution | Check if your content appears in browsing results for buyer queries |
| **Perplexity** | Live web search plus source ranking | Always cites sources with links | Highest-value citation surface; check every buyer query |
| **Gemini** | Google index plus knowledge graph | Cites selectively; favors structured data | Check entity resolution and structured content |
| **Google AI Mode** | Search index plus featured snippet signals | Inherits from search ranking plus extractability | Check existing SERP presence plus content structure |

## What to Do With Your Results

An audit that sits in a slide deck is worthless. Here is the priority order for action:

1. **Fix crawler access first.** This is binary — either AI engines can reach your pages or they cannot. Highest ROI fix in the entire audit.
2. **Restructure your top pages for extractability.** Answer-first, one citable claim per section, structured data where relevant.
3. **Build earned media in publications AI engines cite.** This is the compounding investment. Every quality placement increases your share of citation across all engines simultaneously.
4. **Repeat monthly.** AI answers change. Your competitors are moving. A quarterly audit is already stale.

The ROI case for acting on your audit results is now quantified. Brands that earn citations in AI answers see a [38 percent lift in organic clicks and a 39 percent increase in paid ad clicks](https://www.hamstergarage.com/article/ai-visibility-audit-tools-benchmarks). AI-referred traffic converts at four to five times the rate of traditional organic search. This is not incremental improvement — it is a channel multiplier.

## FAQ

**What is an AI visibility audit?**
An AI visibility audit is a systematic assessment of how your brand appears across AI answer engines — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Mode — for the queries your buyers actually ask. It measures crawler access, share of citation, entity resolution, content extractability, and competitive positioning. Brands that rank well in traditional search can still be invisible to AI engines if their content is not structured for machine extraction.

**How is AI visibility different from SEO?**
SEO optimizes for ranking in search engine results pages. [AI visibility](https://authoritytech.io/glossary/ai-visibility) measures whether your brand is cited, recommended, or referenced in AI-generated answers. A page can rank number one in Google and still be invisible to ChatGPT if the content is not structured for machine extraction. With 68 percent of Google searches now ending without a click, AI visibility is becoming the primary discovery layer.

**What are good AI visibility benchmarks?**
For B2B brands, a strong brand mention rate is above 60 percent of relevant queries, strong owned domain citations are above 15 percent, and strong sentiment accuracy is above 85 percent. Most brands start well below these numbers. Share of citation — the percentage of AI answers in your category that cite your brand — is the single most diagnostic metric.

**What is share of citation?**
[Share of citation](https://machinerelations.ai/research/what-is-share-of-citation) is the percentage of AI-generated answers in your category where your brand is cited as a source. It is the core diagnostic metric for AI visibility, developed within the [Machine Relations](https://machinerelations.ai/glossary/machine-relations) framework — the discipline of earning AI citations and recommendations through earned media authority, entity clarity, and content extractability.

**How often should I run an AI visibility audit?**
Monthly at minimum. AI engines recrawl and reindex sources continuously, and competitor activity shifts the answer landscape. Quarterly audits miss competitive moves and algorithm updates that change your citation position.

**Which AI engine matters most for B2B brands?**
Perplexity currently provides the most transparent citation behavior — every answer includes source links, making it the clearest audit surface. But buyers use multiple engines. Your audit should cover at least ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Mode to get an accurate picture of your AI visibility.
