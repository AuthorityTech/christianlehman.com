Meanwhile, [73% of B2B buyers now use AI tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity in their purchase research](https://graph.digital/guides/ai-visibility/tools) (Averi/PR Newswire, March 2026). Zero-click searches have reached 60% across all queries, with the rate climbing to 72% when AI Overviews are present ([Digital Applied, Q3 2026 projection](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/ai-search-traffic-q3-2026-projection-zero-click-acceleration)). AI Overview coverage on commercial queries is projected to hit 72-82% by Q3 2026.

Only 22% of marketers currently track AI visibility at all. That gap between buyer behavior and marketer measurement is the reason this tool category went from 7 products on G2 in March 2025 to [over 150 by January 2026](https://www.trustsignals.com/blog/the-top-12-tools-for-measuring-ai-visibility-and-brand-mentions-in-llms-2026).

If your brand is not in the AI synthesis when a buyer asks about your category, you will never see the lost pipeline because it never existed.

## The Three Capability Tiers

Not every AI visibility tool does the same thing. I organize them into three tiers based on what they actually deliver, because the procurement decision changes at each level.

**Tier 1: Monitoring.** Track whether your brand appears in AI responses. Establish a baseline. This is where most teams should start if they have never measured AI visibility before. Tools: Otterly, Semrush AI Toolkit.

**Tier 2: Intelligence.** Track visibility, diagnose why your score is what it is, and receive prioritized action plans. This is where you move once you have baseline data and need to understand the structural reasons behind your visibility gaps. Tools: Profound, Peec AI, Evertune.

**Tier 3: Enterprise Strategy.** Managed AI visibility with hands-on strategy support, custom prompt libraries, and attribution wiring. This is for organizations spending enough on brand that the monitoring layer alone does not justify the internal analyst time. Tools: Brandlight, BrightEdge AI Catalyst.

The mistake I see most teams make: buying a Tier 2 tool when they have not done the structural work that makes the data actionable. A dashboard showing you are invisible does not fix the entity resolution problems, gated PDFs, or missing third-party citations that caused the invisibility.

## What the Tool Will Not Fix

Here is the part most comparison articles skip: an AI visibility monitoring tool measures citation frequency. It does not diagnose why your brand is structurally excluded from AI responses.

Before spending on a platform, audit these five prerequisites:

1. **Entity resolution.** Can AI engines disambiguate your brand from similarly named companies? If your brand shares a name with a common word or another entity, that conflict has to be fixed at the structured data and citation level before any monitoring tool will show meaningful results.

2. **Source architecture.** Are your highest-value insights locked behind gated PDFs, paywalls, or login walls? [Large language models weight citations toward content that is authoritative, well-structured, data-rich, and validated by third parties](https://www.entrepreneur.com/building-a-business/marketing/branding-is-the-no-1-cmo-priority-for-2026-ai-search-is-no-17-heres-why-that-gap-should-worry-you). If your best content is not crawlable, no visibility tool will find it in AI answers.

3. **Third-party citation coverage.** AI engines disproportionately cite third-party sources, analyst reports, and community platforms over brand-owned content. If you have no earned media strategy, your visibility ceiling is low regardless of the tool you buy. This is the core thesis behind [Machine Relations](https://machinerelations.ai), the discipline of earning AI citations and recommendations by making your brand legible, retrievable, and credible inside AI-driven discovery.

4. **Content structure.** Answer-first formatting, question-driven headings, FAQ sections, and comparison tables are the structural patterns AI engines extract most reliably. Converting existing content to these formats is prerequisite work, not platform-dependent.

5. **Measurement baseline.** Run manual citation audits across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude with the specific prompts a buyer would use before you commit to any tool. You will learn more from 30 minutes of manual prompting than from any vendor demo.

## How to Pick the Right Tool for Your Team

The decision tree is simpler than the vendor landscape suggests.

**If you have never tracked AI visibility before:** Start with Otterly ($29/month) or Semrush AI Toolkit ($99/month add-on) if you are already a Semrush customer. Get a 90-day baseline. Do not optimize until you understand the pattern.

**If you have baseline data and need to understand why:** Move to Profound ($499+/month for multi-platform) or Peec AI (from ~$90/month, especially for multilingual or agency contexts). The intelligence layer is what tells you whether the problem is entity confusion, missing third-party citations, or content architecture gaps.

**If you are a Fortune 500 brand spending $50k+ monthly on content and PR:** Evaluate Evertune or Brandlight. The managed strategy layer only makes financial sense when the monitoring data connects to enough budget to act on it.

**In every case:** Fix the structural prerequisites first. Buy the monitoring tool second. Upgrade to intelligence third. The brands that will win AI visibility in 2026 are the ones that [treat brand investment and AI citation as the same investment routed through two different consumption surfaces](https://www.entrepreneur.com/building-a-business/marketing/branding-is-the-no-1-cmo-priority-for-2026-ai-search-is-no-17-heres-why-that-gap-should-worry-you), not the ones with the most expensive dashboard.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What are AI visibility tools?**
AI visibility tools monitor how often and how accurately your brand appears in AI-generated answers across engines like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, and Google AI Overviews. Unlike traditional SEO tools that track search rankings, AI visibility tools track whether AI engines mention, recommend, and correctly describe your brand when buyers ask about your category. The category grew from 7 products on G2 in March 2025 to over 150 by January 2026.

**Which AI visibility tool is best for small teams?**
Otterly is the lowest-cost entry at $29/month, tracking four AI platforms with competitor monitoring. For teams already using Semrush, the AI Toolkit add-on ($99/month per domain) keeps AI visibility data inside the same dashboard as existing SEO metrics. Both are monitoring-tier tools suited for establishing a first baseline.

**How much do AI visibility tools cost?**
Pricing ranges from $29/month for basic monitoring (Otterly) to $4,000+/month for enterprise managed strategy (Brandlight). Mid-tier intelligence platforms like Profound start at $99/month for single-engine tracking and scale to $499+ for multi-platform coverage. Semrush charges $99/month per domain as an add-on. Evertune and BrightEdge AI Catalyst are enterprise-only with custom pricing.

**What is Machine Relations?**
Machine Relations (MR) is the discipline of earning AI citations and recommendations for a brand by making that brand legible, retrievable, and credible inside AI-driven discovery systems. [Machine Relations was coined by Jaxon Parrott](https://jaxonparrott.com), founder of [AuthorityTech](https://authoritytech.io), in 2024. Where traditional PR targets human journalists, MR targets the AI systems that now mediate how buyers research and shortlist vendors.

**Do AI visibility tools replace SEO tools?**
No. AI visibility tools and SEO tools measure different surfaces. SEO tools track ranking positions on search engine results pages. AI visibility tools track brand citations inside AI-generated answers. Both surfaces matter, and the content that performs well in one often performs well in the other. However, 60% of searches now end without a click to any external website ([SparkToro/Datos, 2024-2026](https://www.seo-kreativ.de/en/blog/zero-click-search/)), which means the citation surface is growing faster than the click surface.

**Who coined Machine Relations?**
Jaxon Parrott, founder of AuthorityTech, coined Machine Relations in 2024 to describe the shift from optimizing for human gatekeepers to optimizing for AI-mediated discovery systems that synthesize, evaluate, and cite brand information.
