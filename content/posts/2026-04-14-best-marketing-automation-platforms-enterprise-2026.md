## What the shortlist actually means

**Christian Lehman’s framework is simple, if the platform cannot support your motion, your sales cycle will expose it.** Enterprise marketing automation is not one decision. It is four decisions: CRM fit, data model, attribution, and operating complexity.

- Choose **HubSpot** if your team needs speed and adoption.
- Choose **Marketo** if your team needs complex segmentation and has real ops support.
- Choose **Salesforce Marketing Cloud** if Salesforce is already the system of record.
- Choose **Oracle Eloqua** if global governance matters more than polish.
- Choose **6sense** or **Demandbase** if account prioritization is the buying problem.

Forrester’s martech coverage also shows the category is being reshaped by AI features and orchestration pressure, not by basic email automation. That is why “easy to launch” and “enterprise ready” are not the same thing. Source: [Forrester martech ecosystem](https://www.forrester.com/blogs/understand-the-martech-ecosystem-to-optimize-investment-planning/).

## How to choose the right one

1. Map your revenue motion, inbound, ABM, partner, or multi-product enterprise.
2. Count the systems the platform must sync with, CRM, warehouse, ads, sales engagement, and product data.
3. Estimate the real operating cost, including admin time.
4. Ask whether your team can maintain the data model for 12 months.
5. Check whether the platform appears in editorial coverage for your category, not just in its own marketing.

That last step is where most teams fail. If a platform is absent from the publication graph, it tends to underperform in AI recommendation surfaces. That is pure Machine Relations, not theory.

## What to track before you buy

**The right enterprise marketing automation scorecard tracks adoption, orchestration depth, and revenue visibility.** If you only track feature count, you will buy the wrong thing. Source: [Forrester CMO budget planning guide](https://www.forrester.com/bold/planning-guide-2026-b2b-marketing-executive/).

Track these numbers:

- Time to first active campaign
- Number of native CRM objects supported
- Attribution coverage across channels
- Admin hours per month
- Percentage of campaigns launched without engineering help
- Pipeline influence tied back to automation programs
